About SSFSSR

SSFSSR is an indicator developed to measure the extent to which each country can meet its domestic food supply through its own food production. This database was developed in accordance with the 10 Principles for Calculating Food Self-Sufficiency Ratios (10-PCFSSR).

To the best of the knowledge of the Development Research Group, this is the world's first public application designed to calculate and present national food self-sufficiency ratios in a unified manner under common conditions.

Main Data Sources

  • FAO FAOSTAT, Food Balances (2010-)
  • Where necessary: World Bank Open Data, USDA open data, and national government statistics
The 10 Principles for Calculating Food Self-Sufficiency Ratios (10-PCFSSR)
  1. A sufficient number of food items and an appropriate balance among them must be ensured.
  2. Primary and secondary products must both be appropriately represented, and their differences must be reflected in the calculation.
  3. In principle, the same dataset must be used for calculating food self-sufficiency ratios across countries.
  4. All data used in the calculation must be publicly available free of charge and be reliable.
  5. The data sources used in the calculation should be concentrated in one place.
  6. Continuity in the data used must be ensured.
  7. If estimates are included, the reasons for and methods of estimation must be clearly stated.
  8. The calculated results must be reproducible.
  9. The method must be applicable under common conditions across countries.
  10. The formula must be clear and publicly disclosed.

In this database, 64 commodities are classified into primary and secondary products. All are converted into a primary-product basis in caloric terms using PPCR (Primary Product Conversion Rate).

SSFSSRについて

SSFSSRは、各国が自国の食料生産によって国内食料供給をどの程度満たすことができるかを測定するために開発された指標です。

主なデータソース

  • FAO FAOSTAT, Food Balances (2010-)
  • 必要に応じて、世界銀行、USDA、各国政府統計
国別食料自給率計算の10原則（10-PCFSSR）
  1. 十分な品目数と品目間のバランスが確保されていること
  2. 一次産品と二次産品の差異が計算に反映されていること
  3. 各国比較に原則として同一データセットを用いること
  4. 使用データが無料公開され、信頼できること
  5. データ源ができる限り一元化されていること
  6. 使用データに継続性があること
  7. 推計を含む場合、その理由と方法が明示されていること
  8. 計算結果に再現性があること
  9. 各国を同一条件で比較できる方法であること
  10. 計算式が明瞭で公開されていること
