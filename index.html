📖 About SSFSSR

SSFSSR is an indicator developed to measure the extent to which each country can meet its domestic food supply through its own food production. This database was developed in accordance with the 10 Principles for Calculating Food Self-Sufficiency Ratios (10-PCFSSR).

To the best of the knowledge of the Development Research Group, this is the world's first public application designed to calculate and present national food self-sufficiency ratios in a unified manner under common conditions.

Main Data Sources

  • FAO FAOSTAT, Food Balances (2010–)
  • Where necessary: World Bank Open Data, USDA open data, and national government statistics
📐 The 10 Principles for Calculating Food Self-Sufficiency Ratios (10-PCFSSR)

This database was developed on the basis of the 10-PCFSSR — a set of principles stating that food self-sufficiency calculations should satisfy the following ten conditions:

  1. A sufficient number of food items and an appropriate balance among them must be ensured. The number of items included must be large enough, and the balance among items must be adequate, so that actual food supply conditions are properly reflected.
  2. Primary and secondary products must both be appropriately represented, and their differences must be reflected in the calculation. The set of items used for calculation must include a balanced composition of primary and secondary products, and the differences in their nature must be properly taken into account.
  3. In principle, the same dataset must be used for calculating food self-sufficiency ratios across countries. As far as possible, the same dataset should be applied under common conditions to ensure cross-country comparability.
  4. All data used in the calculation must be publicly available free of charge and be reliable. The data must satisfy both openness and reliability.
  5. The data sources used in the calculation should be concentrated in one place. As far as possible, the calculation should be based on data produced by the same statistical authority or by a unified source.
  6. Continuity in the data used must be ensured. The data series should be continuous enough to allow year-to-year comparison and time-series analysis.
  7. If estimates are included, the reasons for and methods of estimation must be clearly stated. Whenever estimated values are used, both the reason for estimation and the estimation method must be made explicit.
  8. The calculated results must be reproducible. Anyone using the same calculation method should be able to reproduce the same results.
  9. The method must be applicable under common conditions across countries. It should not be a method that applies only to a particular country or region, but one suitable for international comparison.
  10. The formula must be clear and publicly disclosed. The calculation formula must be explicit and publicly available in a form that can be examined by third parties.
📚 Calculation Method & References

For the basic concept and theoretical background of SSFSSR:

  • Goro Takahashi, A New Method for Calculating the Food Self-Sufficiency Ratio: Supply-Side Food Self-Sufficiency Ratio
    Springer Nature · https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40066-025-00570-z (65-commodity basis)

For the more practical calculation specifications and data structure:

  • SSFSSR, Version 1.1 — Formal Specification, PPCR Structural Coefficients, 64-Commodity Framework, and 2023 Global Dataset
    Zenodo · https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18889852 (64-commodity basis)

In this database, 64 commodities are classified into primary products (cereals, vegetables, rapeseed, etc.) and secondary products (livestock products, edible oils, etc.). All are ultimately converted into a primary-product basis in caloric terms. Secondary products are converted using PPCR (Primary Product Conversion Rate). When individual supply, production, or import data are reported as negative values, they are excluded from the calculation.

📖 SSFSSRについて

SSFSSRは、各国が自国の食料生産によって国内食料供給をどの程度満たすことができるかを測定するために開発された指標です。本データベースは、国別食料自給率計算の10原則（10-PCFSSR）に基づいて構築されています。

開発研究グループの知る限り、各国の食料自給率を同一条件で統一的に計算し、公開アプリとして提供する世界初のアプリケーションです。

主なデータソース

  • FAO FAOSTAT, Food Balances (2010–)
  • 必要に応じて、世界銀行、USDA、各国政府統計
📐 国別食料自給率計算の10原則（10-PCFSSR）

本データベースは、食料自給率計算が次の10の条件を満たすべきであるとする10-PCFSSRに基づいて開発されています。

  1. 十分な品目数と品目間のバランスが確保されていること
  2. 一次産品と二次産品の差異が計算に反映されていること
  3. 各国比較に原則として同一データセットを用いること
  4. 使用データが無料公開され、信頼できること
  5. データ源ができる限り一元化されていること
  6. 使用データに継続性があること
  7. 推計を含む場合、その理由と方法が明示されていること
  8. 計算結果に再現性があること
  9. 各国を同一条件で比較できる方法であること
  10. 計算式が明瞭で公開されていること
📚 計算方法・参考文献

理論的背景：

  • 高橋五郎, A New Method for Calculating the Food Self-Sufficiency Ratio: Supply-Side Food Self-Sufficiency Ratio
    Springer Nature · https://link.springer.com/article/10.1186/s40066-025-00570-z（65品目ベース）

実務的な仕様：

  • SSFSSR, Version 1.1 — Formal Specification, PPCR Structural Coefficients, 64-Commodity Framework, and 2023 Global Dataset
    Zenodo · https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.18889852（64品目ベース）

本データベースでは64品目を一次産品と二次産品に区分し、すべてカロリー単位で一次産品ベースに統一して計算しています。二次産品はPPCR（Primary Product Conversion Rate）により換算しています。また、供給・生産・輸入の個別データがマイナス値の場合は計算対象から除外しています。
